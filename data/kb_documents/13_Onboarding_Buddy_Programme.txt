# **About the Programme**

Every new joiner at Nexora is paired with an Onboarding Buddy — an experienced colleague (not their direct manager) who helps them navigate the first 90 days. Buddies are the human layer that no handbook can replace.

| **Programme duration** | 90 days from new joiner's start date |
| --- | --- |
| **Buddy assignment** | Automated by HR ops in Workday 5 days before start date — email sent to both parties |
| **Time commitment** | ~2 hours/week (Weeks 1–4), ~1 hour/week (Weeks 5–12) |
| **Eligibility to be a buddy** | Employed at Nexora for at least 12 months; in same department; not the new joiner's direct manager |
| **Recognition** | Buddy contributions noted in Workday performance profile; ERG Shoutout issued on completion |

# **The Buddy****'****s Role — Week by Week**

## **Before Start Date**

- Send a warm welcome message via email or LinkedIn

- Share your Slack handle and invite them to DM with questions before Day 1

- Check the new joiner's start date, role, and team in Workday

## **Week 1 — Orientation**

- Meet on Day 1 — even a 30-minute virtual coffee is enough to set the relationship up

- Walk them through the physical office or share the office guide

- Help them get set up in Slack — introduce them in the team channel

- Answer any immediate 'how does this work?' questions (Jira, Confluence, etc.)

- Share your own onboarding story — what do you wish you'd known?

## **Weeks 2–4 — Immersion**

- Check in twice a week (brief — 15 minutes is enough)

- Make warm introductions to 3–5 key people the new joiner should know

- Flag any team norms, unwritten rules, or 'how things actually work' insights

- Help them identify their first quick win

## **Weeks 5–12 — Independence**

- Weekly check-in (30 min)

- Encourage them to bring questions and challenges — coach, don't solve

- Help them prepare for their 30-day and 60-day manager check-ins

- At 90 days, complete the Buddy Programme Feedback form in Workday

# **For New Joiners — Getting the Most from Your Buddy**

- Reach out before Day 1 — your buddy is expecting to hear from you

- Bring real questions — no question is too basic

- Be honest about what's confusing or overwhelming

- Your buddy is not your manager — you can speak freely about challenges

- Respect their time — keep check-ins focused; prepare a question or two in advance

- After 90 days, the formal programme ends but the relationship doesn't have to

|  | **BUDDY ≠ MANAGER** Your buddy is there to support, not to evaluate. Nothing you share with your buddy should feel like it affects your performance review. If a buddy ever makes you feel evaluated or uncomfortable, contact your HR Business Partner immediately. |
