# **2. Team Structure ****&**** How We Sit in HR**

## **The HR ****&**** People Department**

The HR & People Department at Nexora supports 28,400+ employees across 47 global offices. It operates across three major functions: HR Business Partnering (HRBPs embedded in each business unit), Talent Acquisition, and the People Analytics Team — which is our team.

| **Function** | **Made up of** | **Focuses on** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **HR Business Partnering** | Senior HRBPs, HRBPs, L&D Specialists | Employee lifecycle, performance, engagement, L&D |
| **Talent Acquisition** | Recruiters, Sourcing Specialists, Employer Brand | Hiring, candidate experience, talent pipeline |
| **People Analytics Team (YOU)** | Analytics Engineers, HR Data Scientists, Insights BPs | Workforce data, predictive modelling, HR reporting |

## **Your Role in the Ecosystem**

You are the data engine of the HR function. You don't make HR decisions — you make them better by providing the evidence base. The HRBPs and TA team bring the questions; you bring the answers.

# **3. The People Analytics Role — What You Do**

## **Core Responsibilities**

### **Data product development**

You build and maintain the dashboards, datasets, and models that form the analytics layer of the HR function. Key products include: the Workforce Scorecard, Attrition Risk Model, DEI Dashboard, Talent Pipeline Report, and Manager Effectiveness Index.

### **Stakeholder insight delivery**

You translate complex workforce data into clear narratives for non-technical audiences — from slide decks for the CPO to HRBP briefing packs for regional leads. You write with clarity and flag limitations honestly.

### **Data governance stewardship**

People data is sensitive. You are responsible for ensuring all analytics work complies with GDPR, local privacy law, and Nexora's Data Governance Policy. You never produce reports that can identify individuals in groups below n=10.

## **What You Are NOT Responsible For**

- Making HR decisions (that's HRBPs and HR leadership)

- Managing employee relations cases

- Running payroll or compensation processes

## **Key Skills**

| **Data analysis** | SQL, Python (pandas, scikit-learn), statistical modelling |
| --- | --- |
| **Visualisation** | Power BI, Tableau — building self-serve dashboards for HR stakeholders |
| **People data literacy** | Understanding HR metrics: headcount, attrition, time-to-hire, engagement scores |
| **Storytelling** | Turning numbers into insights that drive action, not just reports |
| **Privacy awareness** | GDPR, data minimisation, anonymisation, disclosure risk |

# **4. How We Work — Sprint Cadence**

We operate in 2-week sprints following an adapted Scrum methodology. Our backlog is a mix of recurring data product maintenance, new insight requests from stakeholders, and self-initiated analytics work.

| **Sprint length** | 2 weeks (Monday–Friday cycle) |
| --- | --- |
| **Planning** | Monday Week 1, 1.5h — prioritise backlog, assign capacity |
| **Daily stand-up** | Every morning, 15 min — blockers, progress, data issues |
| **Data refinement** | Wednesday Week 1, 90 min — groom incoming requests, scope new work |
| **Review** | Friday Week 2, 45 min — share completed dashboards and insights |
| **Retrospective** | Friday Week 2, 45 min — improve our ways of working |

|  | **DATA SPRINT NOTE** Not all analytics work fits neatly in a sprint. Research-heavy or modelling work uses Spikes (time-boxed exploration). A Spike always has a defined output — a recommendation, a decision, or a documented dead end. |
| --- | --- |

# **5. Data Products ****&**** Stakeholder Flow**

## **How a Typical Request Flows**

| **Stage** | **Owner** | **What happens** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **1. Business question raised** | HRBP or HR Leadership | A people question is identified — e.g. 'Why is attrition rising in EMEA Sales?' |
| **2. Request scoped** | Analytics Team Lead + requestor | We define the question, data sources, output format, and timeline |
| **3. Data extraction ****&**** analysis** | Analytics Engineers + Data Scientists | We pull, clean, model, and interpret the data |
| **4. Insight drafted** | Insights BP | We shape findings into a narrative for the audience |
| **5. Reviewed ****&**** shared** | Analytics Lead + HR stakeholder | Output reviewed for accuracy and privacy compliance, then shared |

|  | **PRIVACY RULE** No analytics output may identify individuals in groups smaller than 10. All outputs must be reviewed by the Analytics Lead before being shared with HR stakeholders. Never share raw employee-level data outside the analytics team. |
| --- | --- |

# **7. Data Governance ****&**** Ethics**

## **Our Data Principles**

- Privacy by design: anonymise, aggregate, and minimise personal data at every step

- No individual identification in any group smaller than 10 people

- All data access is role-based and logged — request access via ServiceNow

- Never export employee-level data to personal devices or unapproved tools

- Any privacy incident must be reported to the Data Governance team within 24 hours

|  | **GDPR ****&**** LOCAL LAW** People data is subject to GDPR (EU/UK) and equivalent laws globally. Complete the mandatory Data Privacy training in NexoraLearn within your first 5 days. You cannot access HR data systems until this is done. |
| --- | --- |

# **8. Definition of Ready ****&**** Done**

## **Definition of Ready — an analytics request is Ready when:**

- The business question is clearly stated in plain language

- Data sources are identified and access confirmed

- Output format and audience are agreed (dashboard, slide deck, CSV, etc.)

- Privacy and disclosure risks have been assessed

- Timeline is agreed with the requestor

## **Definition of Done — work is Done when:**

- Analysis answers the original business question

- Output has been peer-reviewed by another team member

- Privacy check completed (no individual identification risk)

- Documentation added to Confluence (methodology, caveats, data sources)

- Output delivered to and acknowledged by the requestor

# **11. Your First 30 Days**

## **Week 1 — Orient**

- Complete GDPR / Data Privacy training in NexoraLearn (mandatory before any data access)

- Meet your Analytics Lead, team members, and onboarding buddy in 1:1s

- Submit all access requests in ServiceNow (including DG-required ones)

- Read the People Analytics Team Charter and Data Dictionary in Confluence

## **Week 2 — Immerse**

- Attend your first sprint ceremonies as an observer

- Explore the Power BI dashboards currently in production

- Shadow a data refinement session to understand how requests are scoped

## **Weeks 3–4 — Contribute**

- Take ownership of one analytics request from scoping to delivery (with buddy support)

- Attend your first HRBP Monthly Sync

- Complete your 30-day check-in with your manager in Workday
