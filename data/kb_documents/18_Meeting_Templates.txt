# **About This Guide**

Good meetings have a clear purpose, a prepared facilitator, and an action-oriented close. This guide provides standard agenda templates for the most common meeting types at Nexora, along with facilitation principles.

# **Facilitation Principles**

- Every meeting has one facilitator and one note-taker — assigned before the meeting starts

- Meetings start on time. The facilitator begins even if not everyone has joined.

- Decisions and actions are documented in real time, not reconstructed after

- Every action has a DRI (Directly Responsible Individual) and a due date — 'we' is not a DRI

- The last 5 minutes of every meeting are reserved for actions, decisions, and parking lot

- Meetings that can be an email should be — consider async alternatives before scheduling

# **Template 1 — 1:1 Meeting**

## **Purpose**

Regular check-in between manager and direct report. Employee owns the agenda.

## **Frequency**

Weekly for new joiners (first 90 days); bi-weekly thereafter.

## **Duration**

30–45 minutes.

## **Agenda Template**

- Employee update — what's going well, what's challenging (10 min)

- Priorities and focus — what are you working on this week? (5 min)

- Blockers — anything you need help with or decisions needed (5 min)

- Development — any feedback, growth conversation, or topic the employee wants to raise (10 min)

- Manager topics — anything the manager needs to share (5 min)

## **Notes go in**

Workday (optional) or shared Google Doc / OneNote between manager and employee

# **Template 2 — Sprint Planning**

## **Purpose**

Agree the sprint goal and commit to the sprint backlog.

## **Duration**

90–120 minutes for a 2-week sprint.

## **Who**

TPO/PO (facilitator), development team, Scrum Master

## **Agenda Template**

- Sprint goal — TPO proposes; team discusses and agrees (10 min)

- Capacity — team members share availability for the sprint (5 min)

- Backlog review — TPO walks through prioritised, refined stories (40 min)

- Commitment — team selects stories that fit capacity; confirms understanding (20 min)

- Parking lot — questions, risks, dependencies (10 min)

- Close — confirm sprint goal, board is set up, stand-up time confirmed (5 min)

## **Output**

Committed sprint backlog in Jira; documented sprint goal.

# **Template 3 — Retrospective**

## **Purpose**

Inspect how the team worked and identify one or two concrete improvements.

## **Duration**

45–60 minutes.

## **Format**

Start / Stop / Continue (most common at Nexora) — or 4Ls (Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed For) for variety.

## **Agenda Template**

- Set the stage — remind the team: this is a safe space; we focus on systems, not people (5 min)

- Gather data — everyone adds sticky notes to Start / Stop / Continue in Miro (10 min, silent)

- Generate insights — vote on themes; discuss top 3 (20 min)

- Decide what to do — agree 1–2 action items with DRIs and dates (10 min)

- Close and check previous retro actions — were last sprint's actions completed? (5 min)

## **Output**

1–2 action items added to Jira; documented in Confluence sprint notes.

# **Template 4 — Stakeholder Update Meeting**

## **Purpose**

Keep stakeholders informed of progress, decisions, and upcoming changes.

## **Duration**

30–45 minutes.

## **Agenda Template**

- Progress summary — what was completed since last update (10 min)

- Upcoming — what is planned for the next period; any risks (10 min)

- Decisions needed — specific asks of the stakeholders (10 min)

- Q&A (10 min)

## **Preparation**

Send a written pre-read at least 24 hours before. The meeting is for discussion, not information transfer.

# **Template 5 — Post-Incident Review (PIR)**

## **Purpose**

Understand what happened in an incident and how to prevent recurrence. Blameless by design.

## **Duration**

60 minutes. Must occur within 48 hours of a P1; within 1 week of a P2.

## **Agenda Template**

- Timeline — walk through the incident chronologically: what happened, when, who detected it (15 min)

- Impact — what was affected, how many users, duration, business cost (5 min)

- Root cause — what was the underlying cause (not the trigger — the root cause) (15 min)

- What went well — what worked in the response (5 min)

- Action items — what will we change? DRI + date for each (15 min)

- Close — confirm PIR will be shared with the wider team (5 min)

## **Output**

PIR document published in Confluence: Cloud Ops > PIRs (or equivalent dept space). Action items in Jira.
