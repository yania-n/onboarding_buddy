# **2. Team Structure ****&**** Finance Department**

## **The Finance ****&**** Accounting Department**

Finance at Nexora operates across three pillars: Accounting & Reporting (financial controls, statutory reporting, management accounts), Treasury & Risk (cash management, FX hedging, insurance), and FP&A — our team — which owns planning, forecasting, and commercial finance partnering.

| **Function** | **Made up of** | **Focuses on** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Accounting ****&**** Reporting** | Financial Controllers, Management Accountants | Month-end close, statutory reporting, cost control |
| **Treasury ****&**** Risk** | Treasury Analysts, Risk Managers | Cash flow, FX, interest rate risk, insurance |
| **FP****&****A (YOU)** | FP&A Analysts, Business Partners, Forecast Modellers | Annual budget, rolling forecasts, scenario analysis, business partnering |

## **Your Position**

As an FP&A team member, you sit at the intersection of finance and commercial strategy. You partner with business units to build their plans, challenge their assumptions, and ensure their numbers tell a coherent story to the CFO and Board.

# **3. The FP****&****A Role — What You Do**

## **Core Responsibilities**

### **Financial forecasting ****&**** budgeting**

You own or support the annual budget process and rolling quarterly forecasts for assigned business units. This includes working with budget holders to build bottom-up plans, applying top-down assumptions, and producing consolidated views for CFO review.

### **Business partnering**

You are the finance voice in your assigned business units. You attend leadership meetings, challenge commercial assumptions, flag risks and opportunities early, and translate P&L performance into actionable insight for non-finance audiences.

### **Financial modelling ****&**** scenario analysis**

You build and maintain Excel/Python models for planning cycles, M&A analysis, investment cases, and strategic scenarios. Models follow Nexora's Model Standards (see Confluence) — structured, version-controlled, and peer-reviewed.

### **Management reporting**

You contribute to monthly and quarterly management packs — executive-level reporting that summarises financial performance vs plan, forecast vs actuals, and key business KPIs.

## **What You Are NOT Responsible For**

- Statutory accounting, audit, or tax (Accounting & Reporting team)

- Treasury operations or cash management (Treasury & Risk team)

- Accounts payable or payroll processing

## **Key Skills**

| **Financial modelling** | Advanced Excel (3-statement models, DCF, sensitivity tables); Python (optional but valued) |
| --- | --- |
| **Business acumen** | Understanding P&L, balance sheet, and cash flow dynamics across business units |
| **Stakeholder management** | Influencing senior leaders without direct authority; simplifying complexity |
| **Data visualisation** | Power BI for management reporting; clear chart design for executive audiences |
| **Attention to detail** | Zero tolerance for error in numbers that inform Board decisions |

# **4. How We Work — Planning Cycles ****&**** Sprints**

FP&A operates on two time horizons simultaneously: the annual planning calendar (budget, forecasts, board packs) and 2-week sprints for analytical and modelling work. Both are tracked in Jira.

## **Annual Planning Calendar**

| **Annual Budget** | September–November — bottom-up planning with all business units |
| --- | --- |
| **Q1 Forecast** | January — first rolling forecast update vs budget |
| **Q2 Forecast** | April — mid-year reforecast; strategic checkpoint |
| **Q3 Forecast** | July — updated full-year outlook; early next-year framing |
| **Board Pack** | Monthly — actuals vs plan, narrative, forward look |
| **Investor Update** | Quarterly — external-facing financial summary (Finance Director-led) |

## **2-Week Sprint Cadence**

| **Sprint Planning** | Monday Week 1, 1.5h — prioritise analytical backlog |
| --- | --- |
| **Daily Check-in** | Daily, 15 min — blockers, model status, deadline risks |
| **Model Review** | Wednesday Week 1 — peer review of in-progress models |
| **Forecast Presentation** | Friday Week 2 — present outputs to Finance Director |
| **Retrospective** | Friday Week 2 — improve processes, tools, team norms |

# **5. Financial Planning Flow**

| **Stage** | **Owner** | **What happens** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **1. Planning mandate issued** | CFO / Finance Director | Strategic assumptions and planning parameters distributed to all BUs |
| **2. BU planning sessions** | FP&A Business Partners + BU Leaders | Bottom-up plans built with each business unit |
| **3. Consolidation ****&**** challenge** | FP&A Analysts + Forecast Modellers | Plans consolidated; gaps to target identified; challenge sessions held |
| **4. CFO review** | CFO + FP&A Lead | Consolidated plan reviewed; adjustments agreed |
| **5. Board submission** | Finance Director | Final plan submitted to the Board |
| **6. In-year tracking** | FP&A Team | Monthly actuals vs plan; reforecasting as needed |

|  | **NUMBER INTEGRITY** Every number that leaves the FP&A team must be traceable to a source. No estimates without documentation. No models without version control. If you are unsure of a number, flag it — never guess. |
| --- | --- |

# **7. Data, Models ****&**** Version Control**

## **Model Standards**

- All financial models must follow the Nexora Model Standards Guide (Confluence: Finance > Model Standards)

- Models are stored in SharePoint: Finance > FP&A > Models > [Year] > [BU Name]

- Version naming: ModelName_vX.X_YYYYMMDD.xlsx — never overwrite the previous version

- Every model requires a peer review sign-off before being shared outside the team

- Assumptions must be documented on a dedicated 'Assumptions' tab in every model

## **Data Sources**

| **Actuals** | Pulled from SAP (finance ERP) via the FP&A data extract — request access via ServiceNow |
| --- | --- |
| **Headcount** | Sourced from Workday via monthly HR data extract — shared by People Analytics team |
| **Market data** | Bloomberg terminal (Director-level access) or approved external data feeds |
| **Operating metrics** | Business unit KPI files — maintained by each BU and consolidated by FP&A |

# **8. Definition of Ready ****&**** Done**

## **Definition of Ready — a modelling task is Ready when:**

- The output, audience, and decision it supports are clearly defined

- All required data sources are identified and accessible

- Assumptions and methodology are documented before modelling begins

- Complexity and effort are estimated (story points or hours)

## **Definition of Done — work is Done when:**

- Output answers the original business question with traceable numbers

- Peer review completed and sign-off documented in Confluence

- Model stored in SharePoint with correct version naming

- Output shared with and acknowledged by the requesting stakeholder

- Jira ticket updated to 'Done'

# **11. Your First 30 Days**

- Complete Financial Systems and Data Confidentiality training in NexoraLearn
