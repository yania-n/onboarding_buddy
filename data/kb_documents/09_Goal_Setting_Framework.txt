# **The OKR Framework**

Nexora uses Objectives and Key Results (OKRs) as its primary goal-setting framework. OKRs provide direction (the Objective) and a clear measure of success (the Key Results).

| **Objective** | What you want to achieve — ambitious, qualitative, inspiring |
| --- | --- |
| **Key Results** | How you'll know you've achieved it — specific, measurable, time-bound (3–5 per objective) |
| **Initiatives** | What you'll do to achieve the KRs — tasks and projects (not part of the formal OKR) |
| **Cadence** | Set annually in January; reviewed mid-year; scored at year-end |
| **Scoring** | 0.0–1.0. Target score is 0.7 — consistently hitting 1.0 means goals were too easy |

# **Writing Good OKRs**

## **A Good Objective**

- Is inspiring and directional — it should make people want to work on it

- Is achievable in the timeframe but requires a stretch

- Does not contain metrics (those go in Key Results)

- Example: 'Make Nexora's Analytics Platform the most trusted internal data tool in the company'

## **Good Key Results**

- Are measurable — there must be a clear way to know if you achieved it

- Are outcomes, not outputs — 'increase NPS from 32 to 50' not 'run 3 workshops'

- Are time-bound — state the target date or tie to the annual cycle

- Example: 'Increase platform NPS from 32 to 50 by Q3 2026'

- Example: 'Reduce average dashboard load time from 8s to under 3s by June 2026'

- Example: 'Achieve 90% of business units using self-serve reports without IT support by Q4 2026'

## **Common Mistakes**

| **Mistake** | **Why it****'****s a problem** | **Fix** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Using outputs as KRs** | 'Deliver 5 features' doesn't tell you if anyone benefited | Change to the impact: 'Reduce manual reporting time by 50%' |
| **Setting KRs that are 100% in your control** | No stretch; no learning | Include at least one KR that requires cross-team collaboration |
| **Too many objectives** | Focus is diluted; everything becomes priority | Maximum 3 Objectives per person / team |
| **Setting and forgetting** | KRs become irrelevant without mid-year review | Schedule a mid-year OKR check-in with your manager |

# **OKR Alignment at Nexora**

OKRs cascade from company level to department to team to individual. Each level's OKRs should support the level above.

| **Level** | **Who sets them** | **Published where** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Company OKRs** | Executive Committee | NexoraConnect > Strategy > OKRs |
| **Department OKRs** | Department Head + LT | Confluence > [Dept] > OKRs |
| **Team OKRs** | Team Lead + members | Confluence > [Team] > OKRs |
| **Individual OKRs** | Employee + manager, agreed together | Workday > My Goals |

|  | **ALIGNMENT CHECK** Before finalising your OKRs, read your team's OKRs and your department's OKRs. At least one of your individual Key Results should be directly traceable to a team or department KR. If none of your goals connect upward, talk to your manager. |
