# **3. The Enablement Role — What You Do**

## **Core Responsibilities**

### **Playbook ****&**** content development**

You create and maintain the core sales content library: pitch decks, objection-handling guides, competitive battle cards, ROI calculators, proposal templates, and case studies. Content is audience-specific, up to date, and stored in the Sales Content Hub (Confluence + Highspot).

### **Sales onboarding ****&**** training**

You design and run the sales onboarding programme for new hires, and deliver ongoing training on new products, messaging changes, and sales skills. Training is blended: live workshops, e-learning in NexoraLearn, and peer learning.

### **CRM ****&**** tooling optimisation**

You partner with Sales Ops to ensure Salesforce is configured to support the sales process. You maintain CRM hygiene standards, create dashboards for sales managers, and identify friction points in the tool stack.

### **Sales performance analytics**

You analyse win/loss data, pipeline velocity, ramp time, and content usage to identify what's working and what isn't — and use those insights to continuously improve the enablement programme.

## **Key Skills**

| **Sales process understanding** | Knowledge of enterprise B2B sales cycles, deal stages, buyer personas |
| --- | --- |
| **Content creation** | Clear, compelling writing; slide design (PowerPoint/Google Slides); video scripting |
| **Data analysis** | Salesforce reporting, pipeline analytics, content engagement metrics |
| **Programme design** | Learning design principles; training delivery; engagement measurement |
| **Stakeholder influence** | Building trust with salespeople who are time-poor and outcome-focused |

# **4. How We Work — Sprint Cadence**

The Enablement Team operates in 2-week sprints. Our backlog balances reactive requests from sales leadership (new messaging, competitive responses) with proactive programme improvements.

| **Sprint Planning** | Monday Week 1, 1.5h — prioritise backlog; align with Sales leadership priorities |
| --- | --- |
| **Daily Stand-up** | Every morning, 15 min — progress, blockers, urgent requests |
| **Content Refinement** | Wednesday Week 1, 90 min — scope and brief new content or training requests |
| **Sprint Review** | Friday Week 2, 45 min — review completed content and programmes with Sales Director |
| **Retrospective** | Friday Week 2, 45 min — team process improvements |

|  | **SALES QUARTER END** In the final 2 weeks of each sales quarter, the team enters a 'support mode' — sprint work is paused, and we prioritise urgent deal support requests from the field. Plan for reduced velocity in these periods. |
| --- | --- |

# **7. Content Standards ****&**** Version Control**

## **Content Standards**

- All customer-facing content must be reviewed by Marketing and Legal before use — use the Content Approval workflow in Jira

- Internal sales content (playbooks, battle cards) requires Enablement Lead sign-off only

- Content naming convention: [Type]_[Audience]_[Topic]_v[X.X]_[YYYYMM] — e.g. Playbook_AE_EnterpriseSaaS_v2.1_202603

- Content stored in Highspot (primary) and Confluence (internal reference). Never in personal Google Drive.

- All content must have a review date (maximum 6 months). Stale content is archived, never deleted.

|  | **LEGAL ****&**** COMPLIANCE** Any content mentioning competitor products, pricing, or making performance claims requires Legal review. Allow 5 business days. Circumventing this process is a policy violation. |
| --- | --- |

# **8. Definition of Ready ****&**** Done**

## **Definition of Ready — a content or programme task is Ready when:**

- Brief is written and approved by Enablement Lead

- Audience, format, and success metric are defined

- Subject matter expert (SME) availability confirmed for review

- Legal/compliance review requirement assessed

## **Definition of Done — work is Done when:**

- Content meets the brief and has been reviewed by SME and Enablement Lead

- Legal/compliance review completed where required

- Content published in Highspot with correct metadata and tags

- Sales team notified via Slack (#sales-enablement) and email where relevant

- Success metrics baseline recorded for future performance tracking

# **11. Your First 30 Days**

## **Week 1 — Orient**

- Complete Sales Product Knowledge and Brand & Messaging training in NexoraLearn

- Submit access requests for Highspot, Salesforce, and Canva

- Meet your Enablement Lead, Sales Directors, and onboarding buddy

- Review the current sales playbook and last quarter's win/loss report in Confluence

## **Week 2 — Immerse**

- Shadow 2 sales calls with Account Executives to understand the field reality

- Review the Highspot content library and identify any obvious gaps

- Attend sprint ceremonies and your first Content Refinement session

## **Weeks 3–4 — Contribute**

- Take ownership of one content update or training module (with buddy support)

- Attend a Sales All-Hands and observe how enablement content is received

- Complete your 30-day check-in with your manager in Workday
