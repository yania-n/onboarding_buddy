How to Use These Plans
Each 30/60/90-day plan is a structured guide for what to focus on in your first three months. Review this with your manager in your first week and agree any adjustments. Progress against the plan is reviewed at your Day 30, 60, and 90 check-ins in Workday.
30/60/90-Day Plan: Technical Product Owner (TPO)
Days 1–30: Learn & Orient
Focus: understanding the context, building relationships, completing onboarding tasks.
Complete all mandatory NexoraLearn modules (Jira, Agile, Data Privacy)
Submit all access requests including DG-gated tools on Day 1
Meet your TCP Lead, FPO, Scrum Master, and squad in 1:1s
Shadow all sprint ceremonies for one full sprint — observe only
Read the Analytics Platform Product Vision and last 3 sprint retrospectives in Confluence
Explore the Analytics Platform staging environment (once access granted)
Join Slack channels: #tcp-team, #analytics-platform, #squad-[yours]
Review the current epic landscape with your onboarding buddy TPO
Days 31–60: Immerse & Connect
Focus: deeper technical and process understanding; beginning to contribute.
Co-facilitate your first backlog refinement session (buddy present)
Write your first 5 user stories independently — submit for peer review before adding to backlog
Attend the weekly Roadmap Review with the FPO
Attend your first Tech Architecture Sync
Identify and propose one backlog hygiene or process improvement
Complete your 60-day check-in with your manager
Days 61–90: Contribute & Impact
Focus: independent delivery; visible contribution; agreeing future goals.
Own your squad backlog end-to-end — no stories require buddy review before entry
Facilitate sprint planning and retro independently
Deliver your first sprint goal as the primary TPO
Contribute to the quarterly OKR-setting conversation for your squad
Complete PSPO I certification (or have a study plan in place)
Complete 90-day formal check-in — agree OKRs for the next quarter
30/60/90-Day Plan: Site Reliability Engineer (SRE)
Days 1–30: Learn & Orient
Focus: understanding the context, building relationships, completing onboarding tasks.
Complete mandatory training: Data Privacy, Security Baseline, Incident Runbook Certification
Submit AWS Console (read) and Datadog access on Day 1; PagerDuty account confirmed
Install PagerDuty mobile app and verify you appear in the schedule (observe only — not on rota)
Shadow two full on-call weeks with your buddy (observe all alerts and incidents)
Review last 5 Post-Incident Reviews in Confluence — understand root cause patterns
Explore Datadog — understand all dashboards in the Cloud Platform Health space
Complete 'Nexora AWS Architecture Overview' in NexoraLearn
Read all runbooks in Confluence: Cloud Ops > Runbooks
Days 31–60: Immerse & Connect
Focus: deeper technical and process understanding; beginning to contribute.
Be added to the on-call rotation as secondary (with senior SRE as primary)
Own a Jira ticket from backlog — reliability improvement or alert tuning — and deliver it
Co-facilitate your first post-incident review (buddy leads)
Attend Cloud Cost Review and Security Posture Review as an observer
Submit AWS write access request (with Cloud Security approval)
Complete Datadog Observability Engineer certification or equivalent module
Days 61–90: Contribute & Impact
Focus: independent delivery; visible contribution; agreeing future goals.
Serve as primary on-call for a full week independently
Lead a post-incident review independently
Propose one SLO improvement based on observed trends — present at sprint review
Complete AWS Solutions Architect — Associate exam (or have exam booked)
Contribute to one runbook update or new runbook creation
Agree Year 1 OKRs with manager — certification targets and reliability improvement goals
30/60/90-Day Plan: FinOps Analyst
Days 1–30: Learn & Orient
Focus: understanding the context, building relationships, completing onboarding tasks.
Complete mandatory training: Data Privacy, Security Baseline, FinOps Framework Fundamentals
Submit AWS Cost Explorer (read) and Power BI (FinOps workspace) access on Day 1
Review the current cloud cost dashboard — understand the main spend drivers
Shadow the monthly Cloud Cost Review meeting with the Cloud Ops Lead
Meet Finance BP who attends cost reviews — understand their reporting needs
Understand Nexora's tagging taxonomy (Confluence: Cloud Ops > Cost Governance)
Review the last 3 monthly cost reports in Confluence
Days 31–60: Immerse & Connect
Focus: deeper technical and process understanding; beginning to contribute.
Own the monthly cloud cost report preparation — supported by Cloud Ops Lead for first time
Identify and document at least 3 cost optimisation opportunities in the backlog
Attend Architecture Review Board as an observer — understand cost implications of design decisions
Complete FinOps Certified Practitioner exam (or have exam booked)
Present one cost anomaly investigation at sprint review
Days 61–90: Contribute & Impact
Focus: independent delivery; visible contribution; agreeing future goals.
Lead the monthly Cloud Cost Review independently (Cloud Ops Lead attends as backup)
Deliver first Reserved Instance or Savings Plan recommendation — presented to CTO
Build or enhance one cost allocation dashboard in Power BI
Agree Year 1 OKRs — savings targets and cost visibility improvement goals
