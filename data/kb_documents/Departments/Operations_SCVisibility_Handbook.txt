1. Welcome to the Supply Chain Visibility Team
Welcome! You've joined the Supply Chain Visibility Team — a specialist data and analytics team within Nexora's Operations & Supply Chain Department. We track, monitor, and improve the flow of goods, materials, and information across Nexora's global supply network.
2. Team Structure & Operations Department
Operations & Supply Chain Department
The department manages Nexora's end-to-end supply chain — from sourcing and procurement through to warehousing and last-mile delivery. It operates across three major functions: Procurement & Sourcing, Logistics & Fulfilment, and the Supply Chain Visibility Team.
3. The Visibility Analyst Role — What You Do
Core Responsibilities
Dashboard & tracking product ownership
You maintain and evolve the suite of supply chain dashboards used by operations leadership — including the Global Supply Tracker, Supplier On-Time Performance Scorecard, Inventory Level Monitor, and Disruption Alert Dashboard.
Data engineering & pipeline management
You work with data engineers to ensure supply chain data flows reliably from source systems (ERP, 3PL portals, supplier APIs) into our analytics layer. You flag data quality issues and own their resolution with upstream teams.
Process optimisation analysis
You identify bottlenecks, inefficiencies, and risk concentrations in the supply chain through data analysis. You produce recommendations that Procurement and Logistics teams can act on.

7. Data Quality & Governance
Data Quality Standards
All dashboard data must be validated against source systems weekly (automated checks + manual spot-check)
Any data discrepancy > 2% vs source must be investigated and resolved within 24 hours
Data pipeline incidents must be logged in Jira and communicated to dashboard consumers in #sc-data-alerts
Supplier-level data is commercially sensitive — access is restricted to Ops, Procurement, and Finance
8. Definition of Ready & Done
Definition of Ready — a visibility task is Ready when:
The data sources required are confirmed available and accessible
The output and audience are defined (dashboard, alert, report)
Refresh frequency and SLA are agreed with stakeholders
Data quality requirements are documented
Definition of Done — work is Done when:
Dashboard or output is live and validated against source data
Automated data quality checks are in place and passing
Consumer-facing documentation (data dictionary, refresh schedule) is published in Confluence
Stakeholders have been trained on or oriented to the new output

11. Your First 30 Days
Week 1 — Orient
Complete Data Privacy and Supplier Data Confidentiality training in NexoraLearn
Submit Snowflake and SAP access requests on Day 1 (both require approval)
Meet your team lead, data engineers, and onboarding buddy in 1:1s
Review existing dashboards in Power BI to understand current visibility coverage
Week 2 — Immerse
Attend sprint ceremonies as an observer
Explore the data pipeline architecture documented in Confluence: Operations > Data Architecture
Attend an Ops Leadership Data Review to see how dashboards are used in practice
Weeks 3–4 — Contribute
Own one dashboard maintenance task or small enhancement (with buddy support)
Attend your first S&OP meeting
Complete your 30-day check-in with your manager in Workday
