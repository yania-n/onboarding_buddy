1. Welcome to the CX & NPS Team
Welcome! You've joined the Customer Experience & NPS Team — the insight engine of Nexora's Commercial Excellence & Quality Department. We own the measurement, understanding, and improvement of how customers experience Nexora — across every touchpoint, from first sale to long-term partnership.
Our work is not just about scores. It's about understanding the story behind the numbers and turning that story into action that improves real customer outcomes.
2. Department Structure — Three Pillars
Commercial Excellence & Quality Department
The CEQ Department drives Nexora's commercial performance and quality standards globally. It operates across three functions: Sales Excellence (methodology, training, and revenue analytics), Customer Experience & NPS (our team — measurement and insight), and Quality Assurance (product/service quality standards and compliance).
3. The CX & NPS Role — What You Do
Three Specialist Roles
CX Analysts
You own the data pipelines that feed CX metrics — NPS, CSAT, Customer Effort Score (CES), and bespoke survey programmes. You build and maintain dashboards, design surveys, run statistical analyses, and produce the regular insight reports consumed by the business. You are fluent in the numbers and comfortable challenging data quality issues at source.
NPS Programme Managers
You own the end-to-end NPS programme — survey design, distribution strategy, response rate optimisation, closed-loop follow-up, and programme governance. You partner with Customer Success, Sales, and Product to ensure NPS insights are acted on, not just reported. You track Nexora's NPS performance against industry benchmarks and set targets with the CCO.
Journey Mapping Specialists
You run qualitative and quantitative research to build and maintain Nexora's customer journey maps. You conduct customer interviews, synthesise survey data into journey narratives, identify moments of truth and pain points, and work with Product and CS to design journey improvements. Your outputs are used in strategic planning, product roadmap decisions, and sales training.
What the CX & NPS Team Does NOT Own
Customer relationship management (that's Customer Success)
Sales strategy or quota setting (that's Sales Excellence and Sales Leadership)
Product roadmap decisions (that's Product — you provide the CX evidence base)
Quality standards and audits (that's the QA team)

4. How We Work — Sprint Cadence
The CX & NPS team operates in 2-week sprints. Our backlog blends recurring programme operations (monthly NPS report, quarterly journey review) with project work (new survey deployment, dashboard build, ad hoc insight request).
5. CX Measurement & NPS Programme Flow
How NPS Works at Nexora
Nexora runs a Relationship NPS programme (r-NPS) quarterly across all customer accounts, and a Transactional NPS programme (t-NPS) triggered by key customer events (contract renewal, onboarding completion, support case closure).

7. Data Standards & Governance
Survey Data Standards
All survey instruments must be approved by the CX Team Lead and Legal (for GDPR compliance) before deployment
Minimum response threshold: do not report segment-level NPS for groups with fewer than 10 responses
Survey responses are stored in Qualtrics (raw) and Snowflake (processed) — never in personal drives or spreadsheets
Customer contact data used for survey distribution is sourced from Salesforce only — never from external lists
Verbatim Data Handling
Customer verbatim comments are confidential — share at account level only with the relevant CSM and Account Executive
Verbatim data must be anonymised before use in company-wide presentations or external reports
NLP theme tagging is done programmatically — manual re-categorisation must be logged and justified
8. Definition of Ready & Done
An insight request is Ready when:
The business question is stated in plain language
The data source and access are confirmed
The output format and audience are agreed (dashboard, slide deck, narrative report)
Any GDPR or data minimisation considerations have been assessed
Timeline is agreed with the requesting stakeholder
Complexity and effort are estimated in the sprint
Insight work is Done when:
The analysis answers the original business question
Output has been peer-reviewed by a team member for accuracy and methodology
Privacy/anonymisation check completed (no < n=10 segment reporting)
Output has been shared with and acknowledged by the requesting stakeholder
Methodology and data sources documented in Confluence
Jira ticket updated to 'Done'

11. Your First 30 Days
Week 1 — Orient
Complete Data Privacy and GDPR training in NexoraLearn (mandatory before accessing any survey or customer data)
Submit Qualtrics, Salesforce, Power BI access requests on Day 1; Snowflake and Medallia on Day 1 (DG approval takes longer)
Meet your CX Team Lead, NPS Programme Manager buddy, and team members in 1:1s
Read the last 2 NPS Business Review decks in Confluence: CEQ > NPS Programme > Reports
Review the current NPS Scorecard in Power BI — understand the baseline
Week 2 — Immerse
Shadow a closed-loop follow-up call with a CSM (observe only — your buddy arranges this)
Review Nexora's customer journey maps in Miro: CEQ > Journey Maps
Attend sprint ceremonies and your first Insight Refinement session
Explore the NPS survey in Qualtrics — understand the instrument, distribution logic, and response data
Weeks 3–4 — Contribute
Own one insight task from brief to delivery (with buddy support)
Attend your first NPS Business Review as an observer
Draft one section of the monthly NPS narrative report
Complete your 30-day check-in with your manager in Workday
