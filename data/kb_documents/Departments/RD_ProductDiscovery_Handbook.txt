1. Welcome to the Product Discovery Team
Welcome! You've joined the Product Discovery Team — the innovation engine within Nexora's R&D / Product Innovation Department. We research, explore, and prototype the ideas that become Nexora's next generation of products and capabilities.
2. Team Structure & R&D Department
R&D / Product Innovation Department
The R&D Department drives Nexora's long-term product innovation — from blue-sky research through to engineering the platforms that power our products. It operates across Applied Research (technology exploration and data science), Engineering & Platforms (build and scale), and our team — Product Discovery (research, ideation, and prototyping).
3. The Discovery Role — What You Do
Core Responsibilities
User research & insight generation
You conduct qualitative and quantitative research to understand user needs, pain points, and unmet opportunities. Methods include user interviews, usability studies, diary studies, survey design, and secondary research. All research is documented in Confluence and tagged in the Research Repository.
Opportunity framing & concept development
You synthesise research into clear opportunity statements and product concepts. Working with Product Strategists, you frame 'how might we' problem spaces, generate concept options, and evaluate them against strategic fit, user value, and technical feasibility.
Rapid prototyping & validation
Working with Prototype Engineers, you build low-to-high fidelity prototypes to test product hypotheses with real users. The goal is to invalidate bad ideas cheaply and build confidence in good ones — not to build production-quality software.
Build / no-build recommendations
At the end of a discovery cycle, you produce a Discovery Brief — a structured recommendation on whether to invest in building the concept, refine it further, or abandon it. This is the primary artefact reviewed by Product VPs and the Innovation Committee.

4. How We Work — Discovery Sprints
Discovery work is structured in 2-week sprints, but the nature of the work differs from delivery sprints. Discovery sprints are hypothesis-driven: each sprint starts with a research question or product hypothesis and ends with evidence that either validates or invalidates it.

7. Research Ethics & IP
Research Ethics
All user research involving human participants requires informed consent — use the Nexora Research Consent Form (Confluence: Research > Templates)
Participant data is anonymised in all research reports — never identify individuals without explicit consent
External user research (with customers or non-employees) requires Legal sign-off before recruitment begins
Incentives for research participants must be approved by Finance (standard: gift card up to USD 75 per session)
Intellectual Property
All concepts, prototypes, and research produced by the team are Nexora's intellectual property
Never share prototype code, concept materials, or research findings outside Nexora without Legal approval
Competitive intelligence gathered during research must be stored in the restricted Confluence space: R&D > Competitive Intelligence
8. Definition of Ready & Done
A discovery sprint is Ready when:
Research question or product hypothesis is clearly stated and agreed by the Product VP
Research methods are defined and participant recruitment plan is in place
Prototype scope (if applicable) is agreed with Prototype Engineering
Success criteria: what evidence will validate or invalidate the hypothesis?
A discovery sprint is Done when:
Research question is answered with evidence from primary or secondary research
Findings are documented in the Research Repository (Confluence)
Discovery Brief is written and reviewed by Discovery Lead
Insight Share-out delivered to Product VP and stakeholders
Build / refine / abandon recommendation is recorded in the Innovation Backlog

11. Your First 30 Days
Week 1 — Orient
Complete Research Ethics and IP/Confidentiality training in NexoraLearn
Submit access requests for Figma, Dovetail, and Miro
Meet your Discovery Lead, Product Strategist colleagues, and onboarding buddy
Review 2 completed Discovery Briefs in Confluence to understand the team's output format
Week 2 — Immerse
Shadow your buddy on a user research session (observe only)
Attend sprint ceremonies and the bi-weekly Insight Share-out
Explore the Research Repository in Dovetail — understand what's already been learned
Weeks 3–4 — Contribute
Conduct your first 2 user research interviews (with buddy present for the first one)
Co-author one section of an in-flight Discovery Brief
Complete your 30-day check-in with your manager in Workday
